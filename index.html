Overview of Modern Board Games

Modern board games are a far cry from the time of Monopoly, Chutes and Ladders and Dungeons and Dragons; there are tens of thousands of different boardgames that cater to various interests. One of the best things about board games is the interactivity that is inherent in playing them; not only are you getting to play a fun game, but you are also spending time with someone in your life.

Right now is the perfect time to get into the hobby of board gaming because there are more games than there have been in the past, and they are more available than ever. If you live nearby a medium-sized metropolitan area, there most likely is a store where you can purchase games and ask questions about what kind of games are right for you. If you don't live in an area with a game store, there are various resources on line that you can access such as Board Game Geek. If you are concerned about the price of board games, Print and Play is always an option.

Different Genres and Examples of Games

  • Deck Building
    • Race for the Galaxy - Build a galactic civilization by playing cards that represent worlds or technological developments
    • Ascension - Build fill your hand with heroes and runes in order to defeat monsters.
  • 4x (Explore, Expand, Exploit, Exterminate)
    • Eclipse - Explore the galaxy and fight for your race to dominate the galactic core.
    • Civilization - Based on the best selling PC game, Civilization is a game where you build, explore and expand your civilization in order to become the largest and most powerful. (See image below)
  • War Games
    • Some War Game - Talk about History.
    • War of The Ring -Play as either the fellowship or the forces of darkness in the world of J.R.R. Tolkien. (see image below)

Comparison to Board Game Geek

BoardGameGeek.com (Hereto after referenced as BGG) is one of the largest websites dedicated to boardgames.
One of the biggest differences between the two sites is the use of css. I have zero styling on my page and BGG obviously has significant styling applied. I'm actually very surprised to see that most of the tags of BGG are ones that I'm using on this page; the exceptions being <center> and <span>. I guess this shouldn't surprise me much seeing BGG is notoriously ugly and outdated for how popular of a site that it is.
